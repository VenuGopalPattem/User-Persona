Saurav is deeply curious and self-driven. He values depth over breadth and prefers primary sources to newsletters or summaries. As a fresher he's eager to prove himself, and trusts the HN community's signal-to-noise ratio over mainstream tech media to guide his learning.

No reliable bookmarking or read-later sync across devices

Can't filter "Who is Hiring" by tech stack or location
Day-in-the-Life Scenario
Every weekday at 8am, Saurav opens Hacker News on his phone during his commute to the office. He skims the top 20 stories in about 10 minutes — upvoting a few, saving 2 or 3 to read properly at lunch. By mid-morning he's shared at least one link in the engineering team's Slack with a short comment. On weekends, he dives deep into "Show HN" posts hunting for CLI tools and LLM wrappers he can experiment with in his side project. Once a month, he scrolls the entire "Who is Hiring" thread and starts bookmarking roles that match where he wants to grow.

He believes a single well-received HN post can change your career trajectory and actively studies what gets upvoted.

Day-in-the-Life Scenario
"
Abhay spends his evenings writing — every 2 weeks he completes a deep-dive article on a topic like "Why my Rust rewrite was slower than Node" or "I built a mini-browser in 500 lines". When he's ready to publish, he submits it to HN at 8am on a weekday, convinced that's the optimal time. He then refreshes the HN page obsessively for the next 3 hours watching the upvote count. If a post catches fire, his blog traffic jumps 10x for the day and his newsletter gains 200 new subscribers by evening. If it doesn't, he spends the next few days wondering whether the title was wrong, the timing was off, or HN's algorithm shadowbanned him.
